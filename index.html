  • Who can Donate Blood?

    A donar must be 18-65 years old and should weigh a minimum of 50 Kg. Their pulse rate should range between 50 and 100 and a minimum haemoglobin level of 12.5 g/dL is required. Men can donate safely once in two/three months while woman can donate every 4 months

  • Who Cannot donate Blood?

    One cannot donate blood if they have cold, flu or any other infection and also patients suffering from cardiac arrest, hypertension , diabetes etc mustn't donate . Blood will be denied if it is tested positive for HIV, hepatitis etc. Pregnant women are not eligible to donate blood . Blood will be denied if it is tested positive for HIV, hepatitis and the of persons who consumed alcohol etc.

  • Can a person donate blood if he/she is affected positive for corona virus?

    The Food and Drug Administration suggests waiting to donate blood for at least 14 days after a positive diagnostic test for COVID-19 without symptoms or for at least 14 days after symptoms of COVID-19 have completely cleared up. Those who have tested positive for COIVD-19 antibodies but didn't have a diagnostic test and never developed symptoms can donate without a waiting period or having a diagnostic test done before donation.

  • How many units of blood can a person donate?

    During a regular donation, you will give around 470ml of whole blood. This is about 8% of the average adult's blood volume. The body replaces this volume within 24 to 48 hours, and replenishes red blood cells in 10 to 12 weeks.

  • Can a person donate blood after having tattoo?

    They can donate blood after six months of having undergone piercing or body art.
